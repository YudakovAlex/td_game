# Rune Siege — The Elevenfold Road

## The story in brief

For a thousand years, the kingdom of Aurel has been protected by eleven great
rune-hearths. Each hearth stands in a different land, from the green western
fields to the mountain at the centre of the world. Together, they form one
vast seal called the **Crownline**.

The seal was built to imprison the Hollow King, an ancient ruler who tried to
turn the living world into an obedient army. His bones lie beneath the First
Gate, but his will has never truly gone quiet.

Now the Crownline is failing. A fallen star has cracked the First Gate, and
the Hollow King's servants are marching along the old roads. They do not need
to conquer every town. They only need to extinguish the eleven hearths, one by
one. When the last flame goes dark, the roads will become a doorway and the
Hollow King will return.

The player is the last **Rune Warden**, a commander trained to build and
defend the old road. At every hearth, the Warden raises towers, gathers the
scattered defenders, and holds the line until the rune is lit again. The enemy
comes in waves because the Crownline itself guides them: every road, bridge,
pass, and ruined avenue leads toward the next broken seal.

The campaign is a retreat turned into a counterattack. The Warden begins by
protecting a single village in the Grasslands and ends by carrying the light
of all eleven lands to the First Gate. The final battle is not fought to rule
the world. It is fought to give the world one more dawn.

## Why the eleven terrains align

The eleven terrains are not random parts of the kingdom. Long ago, the first
rune-smiths placed one anchorstone in each land. Seen from above, the stones
trace the shape of a crown around the First Gate. The ancient roads were built
between them, following the lines of that spell.

Each terrain supplies a different strength to the Crownline:

- living earth gives it endurance;
- forest gives it memory;
- ice gives it stillness;
- stone gives it a foundation;
- fire gives it force;
- water gives it movement;
- sky and starlight give it direction.

When all eleven hearths burn together, the anchorstones align and close the
seal below the First Gate. If even one is lost, the spell has a gap. That is
why the Hollow King's army follows the same route as the Warden: the old road
is both the safest path through the kingdom and the only path that can carry
enough power to break the seal.

The campaign has eleven terrain chapters, each made of nine defenses. The
first maps teach the Warden's craft; the middle maps reveal what happened to
the old kingdom; the final maps bring every road and every enemy to the same
gate.

## The eleven terrains

### 1. The Grasslands

The journey begins in broad green country dotted with farms, standing stones,
and old watchtowers. The first hearth is small, but its light protects the
largest number of ordinary people. This is where the Warden learns the simple
truth of the war: a well-placed tower can save a village.

The open fields make enemy movements easy to read, but there is little shelter
for a mistake. By the end of the chapter, the enemy has learned that the
Crownline is awake.

### 2. The Forest Pass

The road narrows beneath ancient trees. Roots split the paving stones, mist
hides the turns, and the forest seems to remember every army that has passed
through it. Two old routes cross the woods, forcing the Warden to divide the
defense.

The forest folk are reluctant allies. They know the Hollow King once cut down
their oldest grove, and they will help only if the Warden proves that this war
is about more than another king's border.

### 3. The Frozen Road

Beyond the trees lies a high road locked in blue ice. The second hearth burns
inside a ruined waystation, where the wind carries voices from miles away.
The road is long and exposed. Small enemies move like sparks across it, while
larger ones grind through the snow behind them.

Here the Warden discovers the first wraiths: soldiers who died defending the
Crownline and were pulled back into service by the Hollow King's call. The
dead are not invading from outside. They are being stolen from the kingdom's
own history.

### 4. The Ruined City

The old capital was built around the fourth hearth. It is now a broken city of
collapsed walls, empty markets, and towers split by black roots. The campaign
passes through the Ruined Outskirts, the Ruined Market, and finally the Ruined
Keep.

The city holds the truth of the ancient war. The Hollow King was not merely a
monster sealed away by heroes; he was the first Rune Warden. He believed the
world could be made peaceful by taking away the freedom to choose. The other
wardens defeated him, but they buried the truth with him.

The Warden must defend the hearth among the ruins while deciding whether the
old order deserves to be restored.

### 5. The Sunken Fen

South of the city, the Crownline disappears into a drowned marsh. Only raised
causeways, half-submerged shrines, and lantern posts show where the old road
once ran. Black water surrounds every battlefield, and the fog turns familiar
landmarks into false ones.

The fen teaches the Warden that the Crownline is not only a road of stone. It
also runs through rivers and buried channels. The marsh clans guard the fifth
anchorstone, but they have no interest in saving the distant cities. The
Warden must win their trust before the water carries the enemy to the hearth.

### 6. The Stonefang Mountains

The Crownline climbs into jagged mountains where bridges hang over empty air
and old mines run beneath the passes. The sixth hearth is carved into the
mountain's heart. Every road is a bottleneck, and every broken bridge leaves a
village isolated.

The mountain folk have forged the towers used by the Rune Wardens for
generations. They can repair the old defenses, but only if the Warden holds
the passes long enough for the forges to burn again. The Hollow King answers
with siege beasts built from grave-iron and stone.

### 7. The Ashen Barrens

Past the mountains lies a plain buried beneath volcanic ash. Nothing grows
there except red thorn and stubborn black grass. The seventh hearth stands
beside a field of warm vents, where the earth glows beneath its cracked skin.

The Barrens are the scar left by the Hollow King's first attempt to break the
seal. Fire is everywhere, but it is wild and hungry rather than comforting.
The Warden must learn to use the land's fury without letting it consume the
road.

### 8. The Glass Desert

The ash gives way to pale desert where an ancient spell has melted the sand
into sheets of glass. At sunrise, the dunes reflect entire armies that are not
there. The eighth anchorstone lies beneath a buried observatory, and its
broken mirrors point toward the remaining hearths.

This is where the enemy's plan becomes clear. The Hollow King is not attacking
the hearths in anger. He is using the Crownline to draw their power inward,
turning the kingdom's own defenses into a key.

### 9. The Tempest Coast

The ninth terrain is a coast of black cliffs, salt marshes, and abandoned
lighthouses. Storms roll in from a dark sea, and the old road runs along the
edge of the cliffs before plunging into sea caves.

The coast's hearth once guided ships safely home. Now its light attracts the
Hollow King's fleet of dead vessels. The Warden must hold the cliffs while
coastal defenders relight the lighthouse chain, sending a signal across the
sea and back toward the heart of the Crownline.

### 10. The Moonvale

High above the coast is a quiet valley of silver grass and pale stone. The
moon never seems to set there. The tenth hearth rests in an old observatory,
where the first rune-smiths measured the stars and chose the Crownline's
shape.

The observatory reveals the final danger: the alignment has already begun.
The fallen star did not crack the First Gate by accident. It was the Hollow
King's beacon, calling every stolen spirit and broken rune toward the centre.
There is only one hearth left to reach.

### 11. The Crownspire and the First Gate

The last terrain is the central mountain, where all eleven roads meet. The
eleventh hearth burns at the Crownspire above the First Gate, while the
Hollow King's army gathers below it in the old capital's final fortress.

Every enemy seen on the journey returns here: the swift raiders, the heavy
brutes, the armored dead, the wraiths, the siege beasts, and the champions who
carry the Hollow King's mark. The Warden's towers are built on the same stones
that began the campaign, and every surviving ally stands beneath the same
banner.

If the Warden holds until the eleven flames align, the Crownline closes. The
Hollow King is sealed again—not destroyed, but denied the one thing he has
always wanted: a world with no one left free to oppose him.

## The tone of the campaign

The world is old, battered, and occasionally funny in the way frightened
people become funny when the alternative is panic. Villagers argue about who
owns a watchtower. Mountain smiths complain that heroes always arrive after
the tools break. Forest scouts pretend they are not impressed by a good
Cannon tower.

The danger is real, but the story belongs to the people who keep showing up:
the farmer who rings the warning bell, the scout who returns through the
blizzard, the smith who relights a dead forge, and the Rune Warden who places
one more tower before the next wave arrives.

The Crownline is an ancient spell. The siege is won by ordinary courage.
